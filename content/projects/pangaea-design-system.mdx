## Background

As product scope expanded, inconsistencies started to slow the team down. Internal tools were solving real operational problems, but the interface language was fragmented and expensive to maintain.

I introduced Pangaea’s first design system to create a shared foundation for product and engineering. The work covered component patterns, layout rules, interaction conventions, and documentation that could support both new and experienced users.

## What changed

- teams gained a more consistent product language
- design decisions became easier to repeat with confidence
- implementation became less dependent on one-off interpretation

The system was less about visual polish alone and more about reducing ambiguity inside a complex product environment.
