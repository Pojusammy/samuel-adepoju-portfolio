## The challenge

Font licensing is one of those product spaces where the information already exists, but the experience around it is still hostile. Designers and developers are forced to decode legal phrasing when all they really need is a clear answer to a practical question.

I wanted Font Check to feel more like a calm editorial translator than a compliance dashboard. The product needed to create trust without becoming stiff or bureaucratic.

<Callout title="Outcome">
  Font Check reframed a dense legal task into a guided reading experience with quick answers, clearer edge cases, and a structure that non-specialists could actually navigate.
</Callout>

## Approach

I built the product around three layers of clarity:

- a simple initial question that narrowed the use case
- a plain-language interpretation of the license
- a final action summary that translated policy into a next step

That sequence let the interface stay lightweight while still being useful. It also shaped the visual direction: soft contrast, measured spacing, and enough hierarchy to guide people without shouting.

## What I learned

Clarity is not only about reducing text. Sometimes it means keeping the nuance, but changing the order in which people encounter it. By restructuring the experience around confidence first and detail second, the product felt much easier to trust.
