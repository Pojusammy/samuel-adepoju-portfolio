## Why it mattered

Creative capture tools often do one of two things poorly: they either move too slowly for an initial idea, or they preserve the idea without helping it evolve. NoteKeys was an attempt to bridge those two modes.

The experience was designed to let someone record or sketch a thought quickly, then gradually interpret it into something more structured: key, progression, intention, mood, and possible next move.

## Design direction

I treated the interface like an instrument panel rather than a utility app. The palette stays low-contrast, the geometry is circular and tactile, and the interaction model is centered on continuity instead of abrupt mode changes.

## Starter principles

- capture first, organize later
- keep interpretation visible but never mandatory
- make the tool feel inviting to both trained musicians and curious beginners

<Callout title="Next step">
  A future version would explore lightweight AI-assisted interpretation, but only after the manual experience feels trustworthy on its own.
